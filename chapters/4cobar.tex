\section{Adams' cobar construction}\label{chapter:adamscobar}
\noindent In this section we define the cobar construction of a coalgebra and introduce Adams' remarkable 1956 theorem \cite{AdamsCobar}, which (roughly) says that given a based topological space $(X,x)$, one can recover the singular chains of the based loop space $\Omega_xX$ purely algebraically from the singular chains on $X$. We then extend the cobar construction to account for two basepoints by using comodules over a coalgebra, and prove some useful facts about the underlying homological algebra.

\subsection{Definition and Adams' theorem}
Let $(C = \overline{C} \oplus R,\Delta)$ be a dg coaugmented counital conilpotent $R$-coalgebra. The {cobar construction} is a functor from this category of coalgebras to the category of dg associative algebras. There are many references for the following definition, including Adams' original paper \cite{AdamsCobar}. We follow the presentation given in \cite{Rivera2022}.
\begin{defn}\label{def:cobar_algebra}
    Let $(C, \Delta, \partial)$ be as above. The \emph{cobar construction} on $C$ is the dg $R$-algebra whose underlying algebra is the tensor algebra over the desuspension of the reduced coalgebra $C$:
    \[\Cobar(C) := T(s^{-1}\overline{C}) = R \oplus s^{-1}\overline{C} \oplus s^{-1}\overline{C}^{\tensor 2}\oplus \cdots\]
    and whose differential is given by extending the linear map 
    \[-s^{-1}\circ \partial \circ s^{+1} + (s^{-1}\tensor s^{-1})\circ \overline{\Delta}  \circ s^{+1}: s^{-1}\overline{C} \to s^{-1}\overline{C} \oplus s^{-1}\overline{C}^{\tensor 2}\]
    to all of $T(s^{-1}\overline{C})$, which yields a linear map of degree $-1$ from $T(s^{-1}\overline{C})$ to itself.
\end{defn}
The definition of the differential here is valid owing to Lemma \ref{lem:maps_extend}, which says that it suffices to define the differential on the component $s^{-1}\overline{C} \subset T(s^{-1}\overline{C})$. Regardless, it will be helpful to be a little more explicit. By the discussion in the previous sections, as a chain complex, $\Cobar(C)$ has terms 
\begin{equation}\label{eqn:cobar_terms}
\begin{split}
    \Cobar_k(C) &= (T(s^{-1}\overline{C}))_k \\
    &= \bigoplus_{q \geq 0} (s^{-1}\overline{C})^{\tensor q}_k\\
    &= \bigoplus_{q \geq 0}\bigoplus_{n_1 + \cdots + n_q = k} (s^{-1}\overline{C})_{n_1} \tensor \cdots \tensor (s^{-1}\overline{C})_{n_q} \\
    &= \bigoplus_{q \geq 0}\bigoplus_{n_1 + \cdots + n_q = k} \overline{C}_{n_1+1} \tensor \cdots \tensor \overline{C}_{n_q+1} \\
    &= \bigoplus_{q \geq 0}\bigoplus_{m_1 + \cdots + m_q = k+q} \overline{C}_{m_1} \tensor \cdots \tensor \overline{C}_{m_q} \\
    &=  \bigoplus_{p+q = -k} \bigoplus_{m_1 + \cdots + m_q = -p} \overline{C}_{m_1} \tensor \cdots \tensor \overline{C}_{m_q} \\
\end{split}
\end{equation}
The purpose of introducing the dummy variable $p$ at the end of this tedious indexing exercise was to suggest that we should be able to realize the cobar construction as the totalization of a second quadrant bicomplex. Indeed, in the cobar differential, we utilize both the internal differential $\partial$ of $C$ with its differential graded structure as well as the coproduct $\Delta$ of $C$ as a coalgebra.

\begin{defn}\label{def:cobar_bicomplex}
    Let $(C, \Delta, \partial)$ be as above. The \emph{cobar bicomplex} is the bicomplex $\Cobar_{\bullet,\bullet}(C)$ with 
    \[\Cobar_{p,q} := \bigoplus_{m_1 + \cdots + m_q = -p} \overline{C}_{m_1} \tensor \cdots \tensor \overline{C}_{m_q} \]
    and differentials
    \[d_H: \Cobar_{p,q}\to \Cobar_{p+1,q},\quad x_1 \tensor \cdots \tensor x_q \mapsto \sum_{i=1}^q(-1)^{\sigma(x_i)} x_1 \tensor \cdots \tensor \partial(x_i)\tensor \cdots \tensor x_q\]
    \[d_V: \Cobar_{p,q}\to \Cobar_{p, q+1}, \quad x_1\tensor \cdots \tensor x_q \mapsto \sum_{i=1}^q(-1)^{\sigma(x_i)} x_1 \tensor \cdots \tensor \overline{\Delta}(x_i)\tensor \cdots \tensor x_q\]
    where $\sigma(x_i) =\deg x_1 + \cdots + \deg x_{i-1}$.
\end{defn}
\begin{warn}
    We have overloaded the notation $\Cobar$, first to refer to the functor from Definition \ref{def:cobar_algebra} sending a dg coalgebra to a dg algebra, and second to refer to the functor from Definition \ref{def:cobar_bicomplex} sending a dg coalgebra to a bicomplex. We will distinguish these using  subscripts: using $\Cobar_\bullet$ for the first and $\Cobar_{\bullet,\bullet}$ for the second. But the next proposition shows that this distinction is not so important.
\end{warn}
\begin{prop}\label{prop:cobar_bicomplex}
The totalization of the bicomplex $\Cobar_{\bullet,\bullet}(C)$ given by 
\[\tot_k(\Cobar_{\bullet,\bullet}) = \bigoplus_{p+q = -k} \Cobar_{p,q}\]
with differential $\partial_{\Cobar} = d_H+(-1)^pd_V$ on $\Cobar_{\bullet,q}$, is isomorphic as a dg algebra to $\Cobar_\bullet(C)$.
\begin{proof}
    By Equation \ref{eqn:cobar_terms}, we have an equality of groups $\tot_k(\Cobar_{\bullet,\bullet})$ and $\Cobar_k(C)$. Since $d_H$ is of degree $(+1, 0)$ and $d_V$ is of degree $(0, +1)$, the total differential $\partial_{\Cobar}$ has total degree $-1$. By Lemma \ref{lem:maps_extend}, it suffices to show they agree on the component $s^{-1}\overline{C}$. Indeed, under the identification in Equation \ref{eqn:cobar_terms}, we have 
    \[(s^{-1}\overline{C})_p = \Cobar_{p,1}\]
    and on this component $\partial_{\tot}$ is precisely $\partial_{\Cobar} = d_H - d_V$.
\end{proof}
\end{prop}

What is miraculous is that this purely algebraic construction encodes a good amount of topological information. This is the content of Adams' theorem:
\begin{thm}[\cite{AdamsCobar}]
    Let $(X,x)$ be a simply connected based topological space. Let $\Omega_xX$ be the space of loops in $X$ based at $x$. Then there is a natural isomorphism
    \[H_\bullet(\Cobar(C_\bullet(X)))\cong H_\bullet(\Omega_xX).\]
\end{thm}
Thus we can regard the cobar construction as the algebraic analogue of the based loop space functor. As the prefix \emph{co-} suggests, the cobar construction is adjoint to a functor 
\[\Barr: \catname{dgAlg} \to \catname{dgConilCoalg}\]
called the \emph{bar construction}, which is (roughly) the algebraic version of the delooping of a space. We will briefly touch on the bar construction again in \S\ref{chapter:stallings}.
\begin{rem}
    The isomorphism in Adams' theorem occurs at chain level. Moreover, Rivera has proved in \cite{Rivera2022} that the simply-connectedness assumption can be reduced to path-connectedness. 
\end{rem}
\begin{rem}
The name \emph{bar} construction comes from typesetting. Pre-\LaTeX, a tensor $x_1 \tensor \cdots \tensor x_n$ would be written $x_1 | \cdots | x_n$. Adams attributes the name \emph{cobar} to H. Cartan (see footnote 3 of \cite{AdamsCobar}).
\end{rem}

\subsection{Two-sided version} 
The cobar construction of a dg coalgebra is a special case of a more general \emph{two-sided} cobar construction, which will allow us to talk about path spaces with differing endpoints. An analogous two-sided bar construction is well documented (for example in \cite{Zhang2019TwoSidedBar}, \cite{May2025}). Here we give an exposition of the dual construction, which seems to not appear in the literature.
\begin{defn}
Let $(C,\Delta)$ be a conilpotent $R$-coalgebra and let $(N,\Delta^L)$ and $(M,\Delta^R)$ be left and right comodules over $C$, respectively. The \emph{two-sided cobar construction on} $(N, C, M)$ is the cosimplicial $R$-module 
\[\Cobar(N,C,M)^q = M\tensor C^{\tensor q} \tensor N\]
with cofaces $d^i: \Cobar^{q-1}\to \Cobar^q$ given by
\[d^i = \begin{cases}
    \Delta^R \tensor \id^{\tensor q-1} \tensor \id_N& i =0  \\
    \id_M \tensor \id_C^{\tensor i-1} \tensor \Delta \tensor \id_C^{\tensor     q-i-1} \tensor \id_N & i \in \{1, ..., q-1\} \\
    \id_M\tensor \id^{\tensor q-1}\tensor \Delta^L & i =q
\end{cases}\]
and codegeneracies $s^i: \Cobar^{q+1}\to \Cobar^q$ given by 
\[s^i = \id_M\tensor  \id^{\tensor i-1} \tensor \epsilon \tensor \id^{\tensor q-i+2} \tensor \id_N, \quad i \in \{0, ..., q\}\]
where $\epsilon: C \to \Z$ is the counit.
\end{defn}
By the Dold--Kan correspondence we can also regard this as a chain complex of $R$-modules. If $C$ is additionally differential graded, and $N, M$ are dg comodules over $C$, then then we also get an internal differential on $\Cobar(N,C,M)$ in addition to the differential coming from the coalgebra and comodule maps. Taking these new differentials into account, we obtain the following definition, generalizing the previous discussion of the cobar bicomplex:
\begin{defn}
    Let $(C, \partial_C)$, $(N, \partial_N)$, and $(M, \partial_M)$ be as above. The \emph{two-sided cobar bicomplex} has underlying bigraded $R$-module 
    \begin{equation*}
    \begin{split}
    \Cobar(N,C,M)_{p,q} &= (M\tensor C^{\tensor q} \tensor N)_p \\
    &= \bigoplus_{a + b_1 + \cdots + b_q + c = -p} M_a \tensor C_{b_1} \tensor \cdots \tensor C_{b_q}\tensor N_c
    \end{split}
    \end{equation*}
    with vertical differential given by the alternating sums of the coface maps 
    \[d_V: \Cobar(N,C,M)_{\bullet, q-1}\to \Cobar(N,C,M)_{\bullet, q},\quad d_V = \sum_{i=0}^q (-1)^i d^i \]
    and horizontal differential coming from the comodule and coalgebra differentials:
    \[d_H: \Cobar(N,C,M)_{p,q}\to \Cobar(N,C,M)_{p-1,q}\]
    \begin{equation}\label{eqn: twoside_cobar_differential}
    \begin{split}
    d_H(m\tensor c_1 \tensor \cdots \tensor c_q \tensor n) &= \hspace{2pt} \partial_M(m) \tensor c_1 \tensor \cdots \tensor c_q \tensor n \\
    &+\sum_{i=1}^q (-1)^{|m| + \sigma(c_i)} m \tensor c_1 \tensor \cdots \tensor \partial_C(c_i) \tensor \cdots \tensor c_q \tensor n \\
    &+ (-1)^{|m|+ \sigma(c_q) + |c_q|} m \tensor c_1 \tensor \cdots \tensor c_q \tensor \partial_N(n)
    \end{split}
    \end{equation}
    where $\sigma(x_k) = |x_1| + \cdots + |x_{k-1}|$. Then totalizing with total degree $-p-q$ and differential $d_H + (-1)^pd_V$ on $\Cobar(N,C,M)_{\bullet, q}$ yields a dg algebra, the \emph{two-sided cobar construction} of $(N,C,M)$. 
\end{defn} 

This lives in the second quadrant. Once again we are overloading the notation $\Cobar$, but having multiple arguments in $\Cobar(-)$ indicates that we are referring to the two-sided version. Of course, this two-sided construction reduces to the one in the previous subsection, which we will sometimes refer to by the \emph{single-sided} cobar construction. To prove this fact, we need a few lemmas and definitions.
\begin{lem}\label{lem:quasiiso_bicomplex}
    Let $f: D_{\bullet, \bullet} \to E_{\bullet, \bullet}$ be a map of second quadrant bicomplexes of abelian groups with differentials of bidegree $(+1, 0)$ and $(0,+1)$. Then 
    \begin{enumerate}
    \item If $f$ is a column-wise quasi-isomorphism, then the induced map on totalizations $f_*: \tot D\to \tot E$ is a quasi-isomorphism.
    \item If $f$ is a row-wise quasi-isomorphism, and there exists $N\geq 0$ such that $D_{p,q} = E_{p,q} = 0$ for all $q\geq N$, then the induced map on totalizations $f_*: \tot D\to \tot E$ is a quasi-isomorphism.
    \end{enumerate}
    \begin{proof}
        \begin{enumerate}
        \item Let $D^{\leq m}_{\bullet,\bullet}$ be the quotient complex of $D$ obtained by quotienting out the rows above $m$, and likewise for $E$. We proceed by induction on $m$. If $m=0$ we are done, so suppose for $m\geq 1$ that 
        \[f_*: \tot(D^{\leq m-1}) \to \tot(E^{\leq m-1})\]
        is a quasi-isomorphism. 

        Totalizing by taking total degree $k = -p-q$, we have the short exact sequence 
        \[ 0 \to \tot(D^{\leq m-1}) \hookrightarrow \tot(D^{\leq m}) \twoheadrightarrow D_{-m, \bullet}[m] \to 0 \]
        where $D_{-m, \bullet}[m]$ is the shifted complex 
        \[\cdots \to 0 \to D_{-m, 0} \to D_{-m, 1} \to \cdots\]
        where the $D_{-m, 0}$ term lies in degree $m$. Then by the five lemma applied to the following diagram and the corresponding diagram in the long exact sequence on homology
        \[\begin{tikzcd}[cramped]

            0 & {\tot(D^{\leq m-1})} & {\tot(D^{\leq m})} & {D_{-m,\bullet}[m]} & 0 \\
            0 & {\tot(E^{\leq m-1})} & {\tot(E^{\leq m})} & {E_{-m,\bullet}[m]} & 0
            \arrow[from=1-1, to=1-2]
            \arrow[hook, from=1-2, to=1-3]
            \arrow[from=1-2, to=2-2]
            \arrow[two heads, from=1-3, to=1-4]
            \arrow[from=1-3, to=2-3]
            \arrow[from=1-4, to=1-5]

        we conclude that $f_*$ is a quasi-isomorphism $\tot(D^{\leq m}) \to \tot(E^{\leq m})$. Since $D$ is a filtered colimit of the $D^{\leq m}$, and homology and totalization commute with filtered colimits, we conclude that $f_*$ is a quasi-isomorphism $\tot(D) \to \tot(E)$.
        \item We equip $\tot D$ with a filtration inherited from the descending filtration on rows of $D$ and $E$:
        \[F^r \tot_k D = \bigoplus_{p+q = -k, q\geq r} D_{p,q}\]
        and likewise for $E$. By assumption, this filtration is exhaustive and bounded below, so its associated spectral sequence converges to the homology of the totalization. The first page of the spectral sequence is given by the horizontal homology
        \[D^1_{p,q} = H_p(D_{\bullet, q}), \quad E^1_{p,q} = H_p(E_{\bullet, q})\]
        and by assumption $f$ induces an isomorphism on this page. Then by the mapping lemma (5.2.4, \cite{Weibel1995-gl}), $f$ induces an isomorphism on the $\infty$-pages of $D$ and $E$. Then applying the five lemma inductively on the finite filtrations of $H_\bullet(\tot D)$ and $H_\bullet(\tot E)$ completes the argument.
        \end{enumerate}
    \end{proof}
\end{lem}

\begin{prop}\label{prop:twoside_reduction}  
Let $C$ be a conilpotent dg $R$-coalgebra with coaugmentation $\eta: R\to C$. Set $N=M=R$, regarded as a dg $R$-comodule with $R$ concentrated in degree zero. Define the left action $\Delta^L: N\to C\tensor N$ via 
\[\begin{tikzcd}[cramped]
    % https://q.uiver.app/#q=WzAsMyxbMCwwLCJSIl0sWzAsMSwiQyJdLFsxLDEsIkNcXHRlbnNvciBSIl0sWzAsMSwiXFxldGEiLDJdLFswLDIsIlxcRGVsdGFeTCIsMCx7InN0eWxlIjp7ImJvZHkiOnsibmFtZSI6ImRhc2hlZCJ9fX1dLFsxLDIsIlxcY29uZyIsMl1d
	R & \\
	C & {C\tensor R}
	\arrow["\eta"', from=1-1, to=2-1]
	\arrow["{\Delta^L}", dashed, from=1-1, to=2-2]
	\arrow["\cong"', from=2-1, to=2-2]
\end{tikzcd}\]
and likewise for the right action. Then $\tot_\bullet\Cobar(R,C,R)$ as above is quasi-isomorphic to the single cobar construction $\Cobar_\bullet(C)$ of Definition \ref{def:cobar_algebra}.

\begin{proof}
    Consider the bicomplex that is obtained by replacing each column of $\Cobar(R,C,R)$ with its normalized cochain complex:
    \begin{equation*}
	\begin{split}
		N\Cobar_{p,q}(R,C,R) &\cong\bigcap_{i=0}^{q-1}\ker(s^i: \Cobar_{p,q}\to \Cobar_{p,q-1})_{-p} \\
		&= \bigcap_{i=0}^{q-1}\ker(\id^i\tensor \epsilon \tensor \id^{\tensor q-i-1})_p \\
		&=  (\ker(\epsilon)^{\tensor q})_{-p} \\
		&= \bigoplus_{m_1 + \cdots + m_q = -p} \overline{C_{n_1}} \tensor \cdots \tensor \overline{C_{n_q}} \\
        &= \Cobar_{p,q}(C).
	\end{split}
\end{equation*}
Upon checking differentials, we conclude that the normalized two-sided cobar bicomplex $N\Cobar$ is isomorphic to the one-sided cobar bicomplex on $C$. By Lemma \ref{lem:quasiiso_bicomplex}, the map $\Cobar \to N\Cobar$ induces a quasi-isomorphism of total complexes, completing the proof.
\end{proof}
\end{prop}

We hope for the two-sided cobar construction to be functorial enough, i.e. it should send most quasi-isomorphisms of dg coalgebras to quasi–isomorphisms of bicomplexes. As stated, this is not always true, not even for the single-sided cobar construction. An example may be found in Proposition 2.4.3, \cite{Loday2012}. There are two remedies: (1) we can impose a stronger condition on the quasi-isomorphism of coalgebras, or (2) in view of the second half of Lemma \ref{lem:quasiiso_bicomplex}, we can ask whether the induced map on a truncated version of cobar is a quasi-isomorphism.

For (1), let $C, C'$ be dg conilpotent $R$-coalgebras equipped with the coradical filtration $F_\bullet$, and let $f: C\to C'$ be a morphism of dg conilpotent $R$-coalgebras. Since the maps and differentials respect the coradical filtration, $f$ descends to a map $[f]: \gr_\bullet C\to \gr_\bullet C'$ of associated gradeds, where $\gr_r C = F_rC/F_{r-1}C$.
\begin{defn}
If $[f]$ is a quasi-isomorphism, we say $f$ is a \emph{graded quasi-isomorphism}.
\end{defn}

\begin{lem}\label{lem:graded_quism}
    Let $R$ be a principal ideal domain. Let $N, M$ be left and right conilpotent dg $C$-comodules. Let $N', M'$ be left and right conilpotent dg $C'$-comodules. Suppose 
    \begin{enumerate}
    \item $f: C\to C'$ is a graded quasi-isomorphism with respect to the coradical filtration,
    \item  $g:N \to N'$, and $h: M\to M'$ are graded comodule maps that are graded quasi-isomorphisms with respect to the coradical filtration, 
    \item $C, C', N, N', M, M'$ and their associated gradeds are flat over $R$. 
    \end{enumerate}
    Then the induced map 
    \[(g,f,h)_*: \tot\Cobar(N,C,M)\to \tot\Cobar(N',C', M')\]
    is a quasi-isomorphism.
    \begin{proof} 
        We imitate the strategy of the proof of the analogous Proposition 2.4.1 in \cite{Loday2012}, which is over a field and done for the regular cobar construction.  Define a weighting on the elements of $C, N, M$ via 
        \[|x| = \{\min r: x \in F_r\}\]
        where $F_r$ is the coradical filtration on the respective modules. Consider the filtration on the two-sided cobar construction given by 
        \[F_r\Cobar(N,C,M) := \{m \tensor c_1 \tensor \cdots \tensor c_q \tensor n: |m| + |c_1| + \cdots + |c_q| + |n| \leq r\}.\]
        The totalization of $\Cobar$ inherits this filtration: 
        \[F_r\tot_k\Cobar(N,C,M) := \bigoplus_{p+q = -k} F_r\Cobar_{p,q}.\]
        The filtration is bounded below and exhaustive by conilpotency, so its associated spectral sequence converges to the homology of $\tot\Cobar(N,C,M)$. The zeroth page of this spectral sequence is given by 
        \[E^0_{s,t}\Cobar(N,C,M) = \frac{F_s\tot_{s+t}\Cobar}{F_{s-1}\tot_{s+t}\Cobar} = \bigoplus_{q\geq 0}\frac{F_s(M\tensor C^{\tensor q}\tensor N)_{s+t}}{F_{s-1}(M\tensor C^{\tensor q}\tensor N)_{s+t}}.\]
        Because all the modules are flat, we can commute the associated graded with the tensor products to obtain 
        \[E^0_{s,t}\Cobar(N,C,M) = \bigoplus_{q\geq 0} \bigoplus_{a + b_1 + \cdots + b_q + c = s} (\gr_a M \tensor \gr_{b_1} C \tensor \cdots \tensor \gr_{b_q}\tensor \gr_c N)_{s+t}.\]
        Summing over $t$, each column of the $E^0$ page is therefore given by 
        \[E^0_{s,\bullet} =\Cobar(\gr N, \gr C, \gr M)^{(s)}\]
        where the superscript $(s)$ denotes the subcomplex of elements satisfying
        \[ |m| + |c_1| +\cdots  + |c_q| + |n| = s.\]
        In other words, the induced map 
        \[E^0(\Cobar(g,f,h)): E^0\Cobar(\gr N,\gr C,\gr M) \to E^0\Cobar(\gr N, \gr C', \gr M)\]
        is exactly the induced map $\Cobar([g],[f],[h])$. So it suffices to prove that the column-wise maps are quasi-isomorphisms in light of Lemma \ref{lem:quasiiso_bicomplex}.

        To do so, we define another filtration $G$ on each column $E^0_{s,\bullet}$ based on tensor length, with 
        \[n\tensor c_1 \tensor \cdots \tensor c_q \tensor m \in G_kE^0_{s,\bullet} \iff q \geq -k.\]
        This is an exhaustive increasing filtration and is bounded below, so the associated spectral sequence, which we will denote by $\tilde{E}$, converges. The filtered pieces $G_k/G_{k-1}$ consist of elements with tensor length exactly $k$. Because the vertical differentials induced by the coproduct increase tensor length, they are zero on $\tilde{E}^0$, so the $d^0$ differential solely comes from the internal differentials of $\gr N, \gr C, \gr M$, i.e. the $\tilde{E}^0$-page is just the tensor product of the complexes $\gr N\tensor (\gr C)^{\tensor q}\tensor  \gr M$. By flatness and the K{\"u}nneth formula, we obtain 
        \[\tilde{E}^1(C) = H_\bullet(\gr N)\tensor H_\bullet(\gr C)^{\tensor q}\tensor H_\bullet(\gr M).\]
        By assumption, $([g],[f],[h])$ induces an isomorphism 
        \[\tilde{E}^1(C) \to \tilde{E}^1(C')\]
        and consequently on the columns of the original bicomplex $E^0_{s,\bullet}$, completing the proof. 
    \end{proof}
\end{lem}
In practice, however, the notion of a graded quasi-isomorphism can be quite restrictive. Within the scope of this thesis, we will be working with a truncated version of the cobar construction, in which quasi-isomorphisms of coalgebras will always induce quasi-isomorphisms. Let us make this precise. Let $\Cobar^{>n}$ denote the subcomplex of $\Cobar$ obtained by setting all rows below $n$ to zero, and let $\Cobar^{\leq n}$ be the quotient bicomplex $\Cobar / \Cobar^{>n}$. Then $\Cobar^{\leq n}$ is a second quadrant bicomplex satisfying the assumptions of the second half of Lemma \ref{lem:graded_quism}. Therefore, we obtain the following corollary:
\begin{cor}\label{cor:truncated_quism}
Let $R$ be a principal ideal domain. Let $N, M$ be left and right conilpotent dg $C$-comodules. Let $N', M'$ be left and right conilpotent dg $C'$-comodules. Suppose 
\begin{enumerate}
    \item $f: C\to C'$, $g: N\to N'$, and $h: M\to M'$ are quasi-isomorphisms,
    \item $g, h$ are (left/right)-comodule maps,
    \item $C,C',N,N',M,M'$ are flat over $R$.
\end{enumerate} 
Then the induced map on truncated two-sided cobar constructions 
\[(g,f,h)_*: \Cobar^{\leq n}(N,C,M)\to \Cobar^{\leq n}(N',C',M')\]
induces a quasi-isomorphism on totalizations.
\begin{proof}
By flatness and the K{\"u}nneth formula, the induced map on row $q$, given by
\[(g,f,h)_* = g \tensor f^{\tensor q}\tensor h\]
is a quasi-isomorphism. Applying the second half of Lemma \ref{lem:quasiiso_bicomplex} completes the proof.
\end{proof}
\end{cor}
The scenario in which this applies is if we want to use normalized singular chains instead of singular chains inside of the cobar construction. Note that this is different to what we did in Proposition \ref{prop:twoside_reduction}, where we normalized each \emph{column} of the two-sided cobar complex, instead of replacing the inputs with their normalized counterparts.
